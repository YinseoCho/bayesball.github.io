COMPUTE A PROBABILITY INTERVAL

FOR A PROPORTION

 

You first have to compute the posterior distribution for the proportion P.  You have a table of values of P and the associated probabilities.

 

STEP 1.  Sort your values of P from MOST LIKELY to LEAST LIKELY.  Put your sorted values (and associated probabilities) in the table below.

STEP 2.  Fill in the CUMULATIVE PROBABILITY column.  For each cell, the cumulative probability is the probability of that value of P and the values of P above it.

 

STEP 3.  If you want to find a 95% PROBABILITY INTERVAL, find the first value in the CUMULATIVE PROBABILITY column that is at least as large as .95.  (If you want a 80% interval, you look for a CUMULATIVE PROBABILITY at least as large as .8.)

 

The values in the P column in that row and above are your PROBABILITY INTERVAL.
